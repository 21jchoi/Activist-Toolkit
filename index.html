Stop Sexual Violence on Campus
Action must be taken to prevent sexual assault on college campuses.

One in every five women in college experiences sexual assault.

Sexual violence is an increasingly prevalant issue on college campuses. A disturbing number of college students report instances of sexual assault, and only 20% of female victims report to law enforcement. The date rape drug Rohypnol is just one of the many dangers students must be cautious of.

It's not an isolated issue

Cisgender female students are not the only people impacted. 21% of transgender, genderqueer, and gender nonconforming undergraduate students have been sexually assaulted, and 8% of undergraduate cisgender men are sexually assaulted at college.

The problem is not going away

The rate of sexual assault on college campuses has remained high. Factors such as underreporting, victim-perpetrator relationships, and ineffective school policies and procedures contribute to the prevalence of the problem. There is a severe lack of education on sexual assualt prevention and proper training provided to the students and staff across many colleges.
